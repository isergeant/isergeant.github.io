Recruitment

Find the right fit for your organisation

Types of recruitment


Recruitment has to happen when the organization identifies the need to increase headcount or an employee leaving or moving internally to another role and the previous role needs to be backfilled.

Recruitment can occur internally or externally. Internal recruitment is encourage as it gives the opportunity to a current employees to expand their area of expertise, to increase their engagement toward the organization and to show example that growth within the business is a possibility, which is highly desirable. External recruitment happens when the company prefers to search on the job market the right fit for its opened positions. External recruitment can take place via internal recruiters search the web for the right profiles, or, via recruitment agencies which will search the candidates for the organization in accordance with the required criteria.

Recruitment and selection: step by step

Job description

The job description of a role is company's CV to a candidate. It is of crucial importance this is written accurately and providing a good first impression for the job seekers in the market as well as contributing to the employer's branding. 

HR & manager should work hand in hand on this document in order to attract candidates.

Job description elements

  • Job title
  • Organisation name & description of activity
  • Job duties and responsibilities
  • Required qualifications 
  • Desirable qualification, profile & skills
  • Location & work model (office based, remote, hybrid)
  • Optional: salary bracket

Internal and external posting

Once there is a need for recruiting for an opened position, it is recommend to look first of all at the existing staff. Depending on the tools the organisation has at disposal, it can either post the vacant role on its job search engine or advertise it in alternative ways to the current employees and wait a reasonable amount of time for the interested employees to apply.

If no internal application or successful internal candidate is found, the organisation can start advertising the role externally. This can either be done by the company's recruiters or by reaching out to recruitment agency to do so.

Internal recruiters have a better sense of the company structure & values and the approach will be more engaging then via recruitment agencies. Although, for small companies with few recruiment needs, it might just be more convenient financially to hand the recruitment piece over to external agencies.

Bearing in mind the company will have to reach out to the relevant recruitment agency as these are very often specialized in a certain type of profiles (sales people, admin staff, etc). 

Interview process

Screening call

This is led by the internal recruiter or the recruitment agency.

The call should not be long and the questions should be around the job description and checking if the candidate meets the factual criteria (salary expectations, location, qualification, overall experience)

1st interview

This first interview is usually led by the role's manager.

The questions should go more in depth in the experience of the candidate but also, it is a good time to start assessing whether the candidates's attidude will be a good fit for the team.

it is also the opportunity for the candidates to to ask their own questions about the company and team.

2nd interview

The second interview is generally meant to narrow down the interested caniddates.

It is usually led by someone from the team able to ask technical questions and, again, gauge the social fit of the candidate within the team.

NB: the interviewers should not ask twice the same questions as it will look like there is no proper communication between the 1st and 2nd interviewers.

3rd interview

Optional: usually for more senior positions. 

This is the opportunity to ask further questions or to ask the candidate to prepare a presentation on the role topic, usually as part of a panel interview inviting other professional colleagues who will bring a fresh point of view and confirm feedback.


Depending on the amount of application, the organisation might want to submit the candidates to a ver bal and numerical test. These are a way to filter candidates who are willing to spend several hours training and then take an online test. Be careful with these as it might discourage skilled candidates to proceed with their application.

Following this, the interviews can take place. The organisation will have to decide who the candidates are going to meet, the number of interviews depending on the role level, the questions asked, and the desirable answers.

Making the offer

Once the organisation has identified the candidate it would like to offer the job to, the information usually is made over the phone. The organisation will have to offer the candidate a reasonable time to come back with an answer, usually 2-3 days.

If the offer is accepted, the recruiter should request the basic documents for the HR department to generate the paperwork: copy of passport/relevant right to work (BRP card, pre-settled or settled status proof), proof of address, CV. 

Most companies nowadays indicate in the offer letter that the offer is conditional to a successful background check report . The HR professional will initiate these checks after the signature of the offer and T&C's and this can take from a few weeks to a few months to be completed. Usually the employees has already started the job when the background checks come back completed. The extent of the background screening exercice depends from country to country, making such checks illegal in some of them.
