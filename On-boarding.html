On-boarding 

Preparing a successful onboarding process


A well-thought onboarding process is crucial but however often overlooked by organisations.

Organizations with a strong onboarding process improve new hire retention by 82% and productivity by over 70% (Glassdoor, 2015)

Pre-boarding stage

The pre-boarding stage is the time frame between the verbal offer acceptance and the start date of the employees. 
The period length varies according to multiple factors. Usually, the minimum length is around 10 days in order to allow enough time for HR to set up the employee in the systems and getting the IT equipment ready. This time frame can sometimes reach several months, as affected by the notice period the future employees has to observe towards their  previous employer.

We divide this stage in two: Welcome email & Acknowledgement of acceptance and practical information

Welcome email

Candidates, especially when they initiated a job search for several weeks, tend to receive numerous emails from job boards or recruitment agencies which clutters their inbox.

 This is why it is always best to give them a heads up prior sending the new starter documents over.

Make sure this email is sent from the generic HR inbox.

In this welcome email, HR professionals have the opportunity to details the following:

  • What new starters documents will be in the envelope
  • Announce background checks will be carried once this offer is accepted
  • Introduce themselves for any further questions the candidates might have (change of start date, unclear contract clause, etc)
  • Any additional onboarding tool

Acknowledgement of acceptance and practical information

Once HR receives the contract of employment and other new starter documents completed and signed, it is recommended to reach out to the future employees with more details around the process until their first day and first day information. 

Benefits of this communication

  • Reassure the future employee 
  • Reinforce the feeling of being welcome in the new workplace
  • Informative
  • Demonstrate the availibility of HR to assist new starters

Office onboarding

It is essential to communicate:

  • The time to arrive at the office
  • Who they should ask for at the reception (usually the hiring manager or a team member)
  • The dress code
  • To bring their passport/relevant right to work in the country

Remote onboarding

It is essential to communicate:

  • Approximate date of IT equipment shipment
  • Time they are expected to login on their first day
  • Who they will be meeting first when they login (usually IT to help set up VPN access)
  • Contact details of IT technicians
  • Onboarding plan for day 1, if already prepared

First day/first week

It is critical to have a well-prepared onboarding plan for the new starters first week. The onboarding plan is a joint effort from the hiring manager and HR in order to provide the new hires the key information in their journey within the organisation to become efficient as quick as possible.

In the onboarding plan, we can often identify: the presence of a work buddy, and HR induction, meeting with the team member, regular catch up with the hiring manager monitoring the progress, introduction to the systems, etc.

Work buddy

What are the benefits of a work buddy?

  • Helps the new starter to feel welcome, answers questions and help the new person navigate through the organization’s culture
  • Accelerate the productivity & job satisfaction
  • Increases the engagement level for both new starter and buddies themselves
  • Socializing is made easier

HR Induction

What information to provide?

  • General organisation data: number of employees, gender split, offices location
  • Company structure
  • Overview of the benefits
  • Activities/socials to increase engagement
  • Best ways to contact HR (ticketing services or generic inbox, depending on the organisation)

30, 60, 90 days plan

This plan breaks down the achievement that are expected form the new hire after 30, 60 and 90 days from their start date.

Each block of 30 days entitles specific goals depending on the role and these targets articulate around four themes:
- Learning goals
- Performance goals
- Initiative goals
- Personal goals

Usually, 90 days in the job correspond to the probation period length. By following up on this 30, 60, 90 days, a hiring manager should be able to decide whether the probation period is succesful.

For instance, let's describe a 30, 60, 90 days plan for a Marketing Manager:

30 days

  • —
    Learn the tools
  • —
    Be comfortable around the company's structure
  • —
    Achieve small marketing plan
  • —
    Meet all the team members

60 days

  • —
    Learn the interaction between the role and sales products
  • —
    Develop a one month marketing plan
  • —
    Launch first campaign
  • —
    Increase the online community by 5%

90 days

  • —
    Demonstrate leadership and ownership of actions
  • —
    Create long-term marketing stategy
  • —
    Hire a designer
  • —
    Increase the online comunity by an additional 5%

Probation review

Depending on the country and the type of contract, the probation period's length varies (see Countries specific rules).

Towards the end of the probation period, it is critical for line managers to review their direct reports' performance and capture it accurately. As a matter of fact, this documentation often reveals underlying concern regarding employees' capability to carry the job or even behaviour.

HR professionals can provide line managers with a form to fill out in order to assess the employees' profile.
